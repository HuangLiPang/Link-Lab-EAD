Living Link Lab with Ethically Aligned Design

The Link Lab at UVA Engineering is a world-class center of excellence in cyber-physical systems. The Lab conducts pioneering work aligned with IEEE Ethical Design in Autonomous Vehicles & Robotics, Smart and Connected Health, and Smart Cities. Prioritizing impact on real-world problems, the Link Lab partners with industry to tackle the most critical questions at the intersection of the cyber and physical worlds.

Introduction

The Living Link Lab test beds not only focus on combining cyber and physical world but also the implementation of ethical concept between technology and human beings. This website provides the public how Link Lab brings the innovative technology and ethics together.

Ethically Aligned Design

The IEEE Global Initiative on Ethics of Autonomous and Intelligent Systems (The IEEE Global Initiative) has launched Ethically Aligned Design: A Vision for Prioritizing Human Well-being with Autonomous and Intelligent Systems, First Edition (EAD1e), the most comprehensive, crowd-sourced global treatise regarding the Ethics of Autonomous and Intelligent Systems available today.

Notification and Agreement

Before entering the Link Lab, people will receive a data collection notification. Due to the data-driven research, the Link Lab test beds will collect the data from every single person in the Link Lab through cameras, sensors, bluetooth, WiFi, etc. Once you agree with the data collection, you will participate in the world-leading cyber-physical system research. If you have privacy issues and do not want to be collected, you can turn off the bluetooth and WiFi on your phone.

Actor Networks

Actor networks are based on the notion that the relationship between people and technology is constanly changing, where the role of an actor is not limited to a person, but also technology itself, that builds its own network between other objects. Fundamentally, actor networks aim to transform how people think about the relationship between humans and technology when in the development stage of new products.

Users and Non-Users

The theory behind Users and Non-Users by Sally Wyatt presents the idea that technology is designed with specific users in mind, under the presumption that non-users will eventually become users. However, non-users should have a role in the design process as they can provide insight into potential limitations that may have been overlooked.

Ethically Aligned Design - General Principles

The ethical and values-based design, development, and implementation of autonomous and intelligent systems should be guided by the following General Principles:

Human Rights

A/IS shall be created and operated to respect, promote, and protect internationally recognized human rights.

Well-being

A/IS creators shall adopt increased human well-being as a primary success criterion for development.

Data Agency

A/IS creators shall empower individuals with the ability to access and securely share their data, to maintain peopleâ€™s capacity to have control over their identity.

Effectiveness

A/IS creators and operators shall provide evidence of the effectiveness and fitness for purpose of A/IS.

Transparency

The basis of a particular A/IS decision should always be discoverable.

Accountability

A/IS shall be created and operated to provide an unambiguous rationale for all decisions made.

Awareness of Misuse

A/IS creators shall guard against all potential misuses and risks of A/IS in operation.

Competence

A/IS creators shall specify and operators shall adhere to the knowledge and skill required for safe and effective operation.
